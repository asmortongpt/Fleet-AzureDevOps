# Fleet Management Quick Start Guide

**Get up and running in 5 minutes!**

---

## Welcome!

This quick start guide will help you set up and start using the Fleet Management iOS app immediately. For detailed instructions, refer to the complete User Guide.

---

## Step 1: Install and Launch (1 minute)

### Download the App

1. Open the **App Store** on your iPhone or iPad
2. Search for "**Fleet Management**" or "**DCF Fleet Manager**"
3. Tap **Get** to download and install
4. Open the app by tapping its icon on your home screen

### Grant Permissions

The app will request several permissions on first launch. Tap **Allow** for each:

- **Location Services**: Required for trip tracking and vehicle location
  - Choose "**Allow While Using App**" or "**Allow Always**"
- **Bluetooth**: Required for OBD2 diagnostics
  - Tap **OK** to enable
- **Camera**: Required for vehicle inspections
  - Tap **OK** to allow
- **Notifications**: Recommended for maintenance reminders
  - Tap **Allow** to enable

**Why these permissions?** Location tracks trips, Bluetooth connects to OBD2 adapters, Camera captures inspection photos, and Notifications remind you of maintenance.

---

## Step 2: Create Your Account (1 minute)

### Sign Up

1. On the login screen, tap **Create Account**
2. Fill in the registration form:
   - **Email**: Your work or personal email (this is your username)
   - **Password**: Create a strong password (min 8 characters, include uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters)
   - **Confirm Password**: Re-enter your password
   - **Full Name**: Your first and last name
   - **Organization** (optional): Your company name

3. Check the box to accept **Terms of Service**
4. Tap **Create Account**

### Verify Your Email

1. Check your email inbox for a verification message
2. Tap the verification link in the email
3. Return to the app and log in with your credentials

**Already have an account?** Just enter your email and password, then tap **Login**.

---

## Step 3: Complete Onboarding (1 minute)

### Follow the Tutorial

The app will guide you through a 5-screen onboarding experience:

1. **Welcome Screen**: Introduction to the app
2. **Dashboard Tour**: Learn about the main screen
3. **Vehicle Management**: How to add and track vehicles
4. **Trip Tracking**: Starting and stopping trips
5. **Maintenance**: Scheduling and tracking maintenance

Tap **Next** to proceed through each screen, or **Skip** if you're already familiar.

**Pro Tip**: Don't skip the onboarding! It provides valuable context and helps you understand key features.

---

## Step 4: Add Your First Vehicle (2 minutes)

### Quick Add

1. From the **Dashboard**, tap the **Add Vehicle** quick action button
2. Enter the **required information**:
   - **Vehicle Number**: Your internal fleet ID (e.g., "V-001", "TRUCK-42")
   - **VIN**: 17-character Vehicle Identification Number
     - *Tip*: Tap the camera icon to scan VIN from windshield
   - **License Plate**: Current registration plate
   - **Make**: Manufacturer (Ford, Chevrolet, Toyota, etc.)
   - **Model**: Vehicle model (F-150, Silverado, Camry, etc.)
   - **Year**: Model year (2020-2024)
   - **Vehicle Type**: Select from dropdown (Car, Truck, Van, SUV, etc.)
   - **Fuel Type**: Gasoline, Diesel, Electric, Hybrid, etc.

3. **Optional but recommended**:
   - **Current Mileage**: Odometer reading for accurate tracking
   - **Fuel Level**: Current fuel percentage (0-100%)
   - **Assigned Driver**: Driver name
   - **Department**: Operations, Sales, etc.

4. Tap **Add Photos** to capture vehicle photos (front, rear, sides)
5. Review all information and tap **Save Vehicle**

**Screenshot Tip**: Capture photos of all four sides plus VIN for complete documentation.

## Essential Features Overview

### Dashboard: Your Command Center

The **Dashboard** provides a real-time overview of your fleet:

**Key Metrics**:
- **Active Vehicles**: Count of vehicles currently in use
- **Total Trips**: Number of trips recorded
- **Maintenance Due**: Vehicles needing service
- **Total Distance**: Cumulative mileage across all vehicles

**Quick Actions**:
- **Add Vehicle**: Register new vehicles
- **Start Trip**: Begin tracking a trip
- **Schedule Maintenance**: Add maintenance tasks
- **View Reports**: Access analytics

**Pull down to refresh** and see the latest data!

---

### Trip Tracking: Monitor Every Journey

#### Start a Trip

1. Tap **Start Trip** from Dashboard or Trips tab
2. Select **Vehicle** from the dropdown
3. Enter **Driver Name**
4. Choose **Trip Purpose** (Business, Personal, Commute, etc.)
5. Add any **Notes** (optional)
6. Tap **Start Trip**

The app now tracks your route using GPS. You'll see:
- Real-time map with your route
- Current speed
- Distance traveled
- Trip duration
- Estimated fuel consumption

#### During the Trip

- **Pause**: Stop tracking temporarily (for breaks)
- **Resume**: Continue tracking after pause
- **Add Note**: Timestamp important events
- **Add Photo**: Document deliveries or incidents

#### Stop the Trip

1. Tap **Stop Trip** when finished
2. Review the trip summary (distance, duration, average speed)
3. Add final notes if needed
4. Tap **Save Trip**

Your trip is now saved and can be exported for mileage reimbursement!

**Offline Mode**: Trip tracking works without internet! GPS data saves locally and syncs when you're back online.

---

### Vehicle Inspections: Ensure Safety

#### Conduct an Inspection

1. Go to **Vehicle Details** > **Start Inspection**
2. Enter **Your Name** as inspector
3. Tap **Start Inspection**

The inspection covers **8 categories**:
- Exterior (body, lights, tires)
- Interior (seats, dashboard, controls)
- Engine Compartment (fluids, battery, belts)
- Fluids (oil, coolant, brake fluid)
- Undercarriage (exhaust, frame, suspension)
- Safety Equipment (first aid, fire extinguisher, spare tire)
- Documents (registration, insurance)
- Overall Condition

#### For Each Item

1. Expand the category
2. Select status for each item:
   - **Passed** (green): Item is good
   - **Failed** (red): Needs immediate attention
   - **Needs Attention** (orange): Monitor or minor issue

3. Add **Notes** for failed items (describe the problem)
4. Tap **Add Photo** to document issues

#### Complete the Inspection

1. Ensure all items have a status (progress bar shows 100%)
2. Add **General Notes** if needed
3. Tap **Complete Inspection**
4. If items failed, a maintenance work order is automatically created

Export inspection as PDF for your records!

---

### Maintenance Tracking: Stay on Schedule

#### Schedule Maintenance

1. Tap **Schedule Maintenance** from Quick Actions
2. Select **Vehicle**
3. Choose **Type**: Preventive, Corrective, Emergency, etc.
4. Choose **Category**: Oil Change, Tire Rotation, Brake Service, etc.
5. Enter **Description**: What needs to be done
6. Set **Scheduled Date** (calendar picker)
7. Set **Priority**: Low, Normal, High, Urgent, or Critical
8. Add **Estimated Cost** (optional)
9. Tap **Save**

#### View Maintenance Calendar

- Maintenance Tab > Calendar icon
- See all scheduled maintenance by date
- Blue dot: Scheduled
- Orange dot: Overdue
- Green dot: Completed

#### Update Maintenance

1. Open the maintenance record
2. Change **Status**: Scheduled → In Progress → Completed
3. For completed maintenance:
   - Enter **Actual Cost**
   - Add **Completion Notes**
   - Upload **Receipt** photo
   - Update **Odometer Reading**
4. Tap **Save**

**Reminders**: The app notifies you 7 days before maintenance is due!

## Tips for Success

### 1. Keep Information Current
- Update mileage regularly for accurate tracking
- Update fuel levels after refueling
- Mark vehicles as "Idle" or "In Service" when not active

### 2. Use Trip Tracking Consistently
- Start trips before driving for complete records
- Stop trips promptly when done
- Enable auto-trip detection in Settings for automatic tracking

### 3. Stay Ahead of Maintenance
- Schedule maintenance as soon as you're aware of it
- Don't ignore notifications
- Complete inspections before long trips
- Keep receipts and attach to maintenance records

### 4. Take Advantage of Offline Mode
- The app works without internet
- Perfect for remote areas
- Data syncs automatically when back online
- Pull down to refresh after reconnecting

### 5. Customize to Your Workflow
- Adjust units (miles/kilometers) in Settings
- Set up maintenance reminder timing
- Configure notification preferences
- Enable biometric login for quick access

### 6. Document Everything
- Take photos during inspections
- Add notes to trips and maintenance
- Attach receipts for expense tracking
- Photos help with insurance claims and resale value

### 7. Use OBD2 for Better Insights
- Real-time engine data
- Early problem detection via diagnostic codes
- Accurate fuel consumption tracking
- Monitor vehicle health trends

### 8. Regular Syncing
- Sync data when you have WiFi
- Don't accumulate too many offline changes
- Regular syncs prevent conflicts
- Backup your data periodically
