### How 3D Visualization Works (Technical Explanation)

**YOU SAY:**
> "Let me explain how this works, because it's important to understand what's real versus what's concept.
>
> **STEP 1: Photo Capture**
> When a vehicle is damaged—let's say there's a collision, a scratch, a dent—your driver uses their smartphone to take 3-6 photos of the damage from different angles. They upload those photos through a mobile app or email them to a designated address.
>
> **STEP 2: AI Processing**
> Our system uses an AI model called TripoSR, developed by Stability AI and Tripo AI, to convert those 2D photos into a 3D mesh model. This is photogrammetry—the same technology used in video game development and crime scene reconstruction.
>
> The AI analyzes:
> - Depth perception from multiple photo angles
> - Lighting and shadows to understand contours
> - Texture mapping to recreate the surface appearance
>
> **STEP 3: 3D Model Generation**
> Within 60-90 seconds, you have a navigable 3D model. Not a cartoon or illustration—an actual 3D reconstruction of the damaged area.
>
> [DRAG TO ROTATE THE 3D VEHICLE MODEL]
>
> Your mechanic can:
> - Rotate the vehicle 360 degrees
> - Zoom in on specific areas
> - Measure approximate damage dimensions
> - Assess whether damage is cosmetic or structural
>
> **IMPORTANT HONESTY STATEMENT:**
> I want to be clear—this is not a perfect 1:1 recreation like a CT scan. The accuracy depends on photo quality and lighting conditions. But it's significantly better than looking at 2D photos, and infinitely better than working completely blind until the vehicle arrives.
>
> We're actively improving the AI model, and accuracy gets better with each update. Some of our beta testers are reporting 85-90% accuracy in damage location and severity assessment."
