## SECTION 4: Q&A DISCUSSION (0:50-1:05)

### Facilitation Approach

**Be conversational, not defensive:**
- "Great question - here's how we handle that..."
- "That's a fair concern - let me explain..."
- "I don't know the answer to that - let me find out and get back to you"

### Anticipated Questions & Honest Answers

#### "How does this integrate with our existing Samsara system?"

**Answer:**
> "Direct API integration. All your GPS data, fuel levels, harsh events, and dash cam footage flow into our platform in real-time. You keep your Samsara devices - no hardware replacement needed. We're not asking you to throw away that investment."

#### "What about our current fuel management app?"

**Answer:**
> "Our platform would replace it. We do everything your fuel app does, plus more - AI-powered price optimization, integration with your maintenance records, better reporting. One less system to manage."

#### "Can we handle both internal shop work and vendor services?"

**Answer:**
> "Absolutely. That's exactly what we designed for. Assign work orders to your technicians OR to vendors. Track labor and parts either way. See cost breakdown: internal vs. outsourced. Total visibility."

#### "How long does implementation take?"

**Answer:**
> "For a fleet your size, we'd recommend a phased approach over 6 months. Start with 500 vehicles in one department, prove value, then expand. But honestly, that timeline depends on your IT department's availability and how quickly you want to migrate data."

#### "What about training? Our staff isn't super tech-savvy."

**Answer:**
> "We provide hands-on training at your facilities - we're local, so we can be on-site. The interface is designed to be intuitive, but we don't just 'train and leave' - we stay involved until your team is comfortable."

#### "We're locked into a Samsara contract. Can we still use this?"

**Answer:**
> "Yes. We integrate with Samsara, not replace it. You can use both simultaneously. When your Samsara contract is up, you can decide if you want to keep it or move to different hardware - but that's your choice, not a requirement."

#### "How much does this cost?"

**Answer (First Meeting - Don't Quote Yet):**
> "Great question. Before we talk numbers, I want to make sure we understand your specific needs. What I can tell you is we're significantly more cost-effective than comparable systems on the market - 20-40% lower - while offering more features. But let's schedule a follow-up where we can present a detailed proposal tailored to Tallahassee's requirements."

**If they push:**
> "Ballpark for a fleet your size, we're talking low-to-mid six figures annually, with a one-time implementation cost. But I'd rather give you accurate numbers after we understand your full requirements than throw out a number today that might not be right."

#### "What if it doesn't work out? Are we locked in?"

**Answer:**
> "No long-term lock-in. We believe in earning your business through performance. We'd recommend starting with a pilot program - 500 vehicles, 90 days - to validate before committing to the full fleet. And your data is always yours to export."

#### "Are you a startup? What happens if you go out of business?"

**Honest Answer:**
> "Fair question. Yes, we're a new company (founded 2024), but we're built on enterprise-grade infrastructure (Microsoft Azure) with professional development standards. If business continuity is a concern, we can discuss source code escrow arrangements or SaaS guarantees. Our goal is to be Tallahassee's long-term partner, not just a vendor."

#### "Can we see a demo with our actual data?"

**Answer:**
> "Absolutely. That would be a great next step. We can set up a test environment with sample data that mirrors your fleet structure - departments, vehicle types, typical workflows. Then you can really see how it would work for Tallahassee specifically."
