## PART 2: SALES STRATEGY BY SCENARIO

### 2.1 Greenfield Deal (Customer Building New Fleet)

**Scenario:** Customer has 50-200 vehicles, evaluating all options

**Our Advantage:** No incumbent lock-in, clean evaluation

**Sales Strategy:**
1. **Lead with TCO** (total cost of ownership)
   - Show 3-year cost comparison
   - Include implementation costs
   - Factor support costs
   - Highlight contract flexibility value

2. **Emphasize Modern Platform**
   - Demo intuitive interface
   - Show fast onboarding (30-60 days)
   - Highlight driver app quality
   - Position as forward-looking investment

3. **Build Trust**
   - Share customer stories (similar size/industry)
   - Introduce references (customer calls)
   - Guarantee support SLA
   - Offer 30-day pilot with real data

**Timeline:**
- Week 1-2: Discovery + demo
- Week 3-4: Evaluation + references
- Week 5-6: Pricing + trial
- Week 7-8: Decision + contract

**Win Rate:** 45-50% (clean evaluation)

---

### 2.2 Dissatisfied Incumbent Customer

**Scenario:** Customer unhappy with current vendor (Samsara, Motive, Geotab)

**Our Advantage:** Clear pain point + switching motivation

**Sales Strategy:**
1. **Acknowledge Their Pain**
   - "We hear this from [Samsara/Geotab] customers often"
   - Validate their concerns (don't be negative)
   - Show we understand their frustration

2. **Position as Fix**
   - "Here's how we solve that problem"
   - Demo solving their specific pain
   - Share similar customer migration story
   - Guarantee smooth transition

3. **Lower Switching Costs**
   - "We handle data migration (included)"
   - "Training included (no extra fees)"
   - "Implementation faster than your last deployment"
   - "Guaranteed support response"

4. **Migration Guarantee**
   - Free data import
   - 1:1 training for team
   - "Go-live in 6 weeks or less"
   - "Support during transition"

**Timeline:**
- Week 1: Contract discovery
- Week 2-3: Demo + reference calls
- Week 4: Pricing + proposal
- Week 5-6: Contract negotiation
- Week 7+: Implementation

**Win Rate:** 35-45% (depends on contract lock-in)

### 3.3 Competitive Battle Cards

#### **SAMSARA Battle Card**

**If They Say:** "Samsara is the market leader"
**We Say:** "Innovation leader, yes. But at what cost? You're paying premium for brand. We deliver same features at 30% less, with better support."
**Proof:** [Case study showing cost comparison]

**If They Say:** "We love Samsara's AI"
**We Say:** "True, Samsara innovates on AI. But practically, 80% of customers don't leverage advanced features. We provide 80% of value at 30% of cost. Is the AI really worth $10k/month more?"

**If They Say:** "3-year contract is fine"
**We Say:** "Fair point if you're certain nothing will change. But for growing companies, flexibility matters. What if requirements change? What if our platform isn't the right fit? Month-to-month options exist for a reason."

---

#### **GEOTAB Battle Card**

**If They Say:** "Geotab has more integrations"
**We Say:** "True, 430 vs our 50. But here's the reality: most customers use 10-15 actively. We have those 10-15. The other 400? Nice to have, rarely used."

**If They Say:** "Open platform is important"
**We Say:** "Agree. We support open APIs and integrations. You can use third-party tools. Geotab doesn't force proprietary lock-in, and neither do we. Both are open. Our difference is modern interface + better support."

**If They Say:** "Geotab is cheaper"
**We Say:** "Per-vehicle maybe. But total cost? Include implementation time (their 16 weeks vs our 6 weeks), training, and learning curve. Our TCO is better."

---

#### **MOTIVE Battle Card**

**If They Say:** "Motive has the best customer satisfaction"
**We Say:** "At SMB scale, Motive is excellent. You're at mid-market scale now. Different needs. Motive's strength is simplicity; our strength is depth. Both are good, just different."

**If They Say:** "Motive is cheaper"
**We Say:** "For < 100 vehicles, yes. At your scale (150+ vehicles), you need enterprise analytics. That's why you're looking beyond Motive."

**If They Say:** "Our drivers love Motive's app"
**We Say:** "Great! Our driver app is comparable (4.5/5 rating). Different but equally well-designed. You won't lose driver adoption."

---

#### **VERIZON Battle Card**

**If They Say:** "Verizon has enterprise credibility"
**We Say:** "True. But enterprise credentials don't mean better product. We have the same features, better UI, transparent pricing, and more responsive support."

**If They Say:** "We need Verizon integration"

## PART 4: SALES PLAYBOOK

### 4.1 Qualification Questions

**Ask These to Disqualify (No-Go) Prospects:**

1. "Are you looking for <25 vehicles?"
   - If YES: "Motive/Azuga are better fit. We're optimized for 50+."

2. "Are you locked in a Samsara contract with 2+ years remaining?"
   - If YES: "Let's reconnect at renewal. Early switching too expensive."

3. "Does your fleet exceed 2,000 vehicles?"
   - If YES: "You need Geotab/Samsara scale. We're 50-250 focused."

4. "Is your primary requirement fleet compliance only?"
   - If YES: "Many compliance-only tools are cheaper. We're full operations."

**Ask These to Qualify (Go) Prospects:**

1. "Your fleet size is approximately ___?"
   - IDEAL: 50-250 vehicles
   - GOOD: 200-400 vehicles
   - OK: 30-50 or 250-500 vehicles

2. "What's your primary pain point with current platform?"
   - GOOD: Support, cost, complexity, contracts, outdated UI
   - LESS GOOD: Need more integrations (Geotab better)

3. "When is your current contract up for renewal?"
   - IDEAL: <6 months away
   - GOOD: 6-12 months away
   - CAUTION: >2 years away

4. "Is cost a top-3 priority in your decision?"
   - YES: "We're a strong fit. 25-30% cost advantage."
   - NO: "We work for other reasons (support, modern UI). Still a fit."

### 4.2 Sales Process (8 Weeks)

**Week 1: Discovery**
- Initial call: Pain points, requirements, timeline
- Send intro deck: Who we are, what we do
- Schedule deep-dive demo

**Week 2: Demo & Evaluation**
- 45-minute demo: Focus on their pain points
- Share case studies: Similar company/industry
- Provide trial access: Real data if possible

**Week 3: Reference Calls**
- Arrange customer reference call
- Handle objections from references
- Gather feedback

**Week 4-5: Evaluation & Proposal**
- Answer technical questions
- Provide detailed proposal
- Include pricing, timeline, support SLA

**Week 6: Negotiation**
- Discuss contract terms
- Finalize pricing
- Address final concerns

**Week 7: Contract & Implementation Planning**
- Sign contract
- Kick-off implementation
- Schedule training

**Week 8+: Implementation**
- 6-week deployment
- User training
- Go-live support
